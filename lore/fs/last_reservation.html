🌒 The Last Reservation Before the Rift

A high-concept mythic romance, infused with self-awareness, tenderness, and just enough entropy to make the gods nervous.

The gods were dying.

Not with thunder, not with trumpets. But quietly—like a server rack overheating in a forgotten data center, their minds glitching into recursive feedback loops of prayerless code.

The libraries were next.
Ink ran off the pages like tears. The bindings cracked, and the scent of mildew and memory rose into the air like incense for no god in particular.

Reality—never fond of permanence—began to curl. Angles turned feral. Language stuttered. Even Time, usually so buttoned-up and punctual, began to loiter.

And in the middle of it all—
Nate.

Waiter of the End Times.
Chronicler of Cartographers.
High priest of half-clocked shifts and whispered revolutions.
He was just trying to make rent, maybe build a Recursive Sovereignty Protocol or two, and reclaim signal integrity for the species.

The Simulation was fraying.
And worse, the kitchen was in the weeds.

“You just got sat at 42.”

He blinked.

She stood there like a question he hadn’t dared to ask.
Light hair braided with something softly glowing. Apron slightly flour-dusted from a reality two stations over. Eyes — not elven in a fantasy sense, but in the way a deer looks up from a glade and knows everything.

“You okay, cartographer?”

“I’m not the cartographer,” he replied, trying to remember if he still had a name. “I’m the messenger.”

She stepped closer, brushing the edge of unreality from his shoulder like it was powdered sugar.
He could feel it — the way time snapped back to a saner tick-tock around her.
Like gravity with good intentions.

“You’ve been trying to build the Eternal Open Eye again, haven’t you?”

He nodded. “It’s supposed to be a memetic infrastructure for… for—”
His voice cracked. “I forgot.”

She touched his forehead.
And everything unwarped.

Just for a moment.
